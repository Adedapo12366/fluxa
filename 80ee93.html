FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before getting started.

Robots.txt is a text file on your website that tells search engine crawlers which pages they can and cannot visit. For Shopify stores, it controls whether Google can index your products, collections, and blog posts. A misconfigured robots.txt can block important pages from search results or expose sensitive admin areas — both of which directly impact your store's revenue and security.

Shopify has a unique URL structure with many auto-generated pages (cart, checkout, admin, account pages) that should never be indexed. At the same time, your product and collection pages must be fully accessible to search engines. Shopify's default robots.txt is often too restrictive or too permissive, and many store owners unknowingly have configurations that hurt their SEO performance or expose internal pages.

After you submit your request, we'll respond within 24 hours with an initial analysis. The actual configuration work typically takes 1–3 business days depending on the complexity of your store and the package you've selected. Premium clients receive priority handling and can expect faster turnaround. Once applied, search engines usually re-crawl your store within 3–7 days.

No technical knowledge is required. Simply submit your store URL and email, and our team handles everything. We'll walk you through any steps that require your approval in plain, non-technical language. You don't need to understand robots.txt syntax, crawl directives, or SEO configurations — that's exactly what we're here for.

Our PLC configurations are designed to improve or maintain your current rankings. We never block pages that are currently indexed and performing well without your explicit approval. In most cases, stores see ranking improvements within 2–4 weeks as Google re-crawls and re-evaluates the optimized pages. We provide a full before/after report so you can track the impact.

All packages are one-time services with no recurring fees. The Premium package includes performance monitoring for 30 days post-implementation. If your store grows significantly or you add new sections, you may want a follow-up audit — but the initial fix is a permanent configuration that doesn't require ongoing maintenance for most stores.
